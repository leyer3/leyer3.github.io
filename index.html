a. Layer 1 and Layer 2:
Blockchain networks are often categorized into different layers. Layer 1 refers to the underlying blockchain protocol, such as Bitcoin or Ethereum, which establishes the foundation for decentralized transactions and consensus. Layer 2 solutions, on the other hand, are built on top of Layer 1 and provide scalability and efficiency improvements by processing transactions off-chain.

b. Layer 3:
Layer 3 solutions are an emerging concept that builds upon Layer 2 advancements. These solutions focus on enhancing user experience, interoperability, and scalability even further. Layer 3 protocols aim to provide a seamless and intuitive environment for developers and users to interact with decentralized applications.
